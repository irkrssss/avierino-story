Preface

From Authors

Initially, this project was conceived as a personal initiative ‚Äî an attempt to restore lost connections and fill gaps in the history of one family.

But as we delved deeper into archives, the geography of our search expanded rapidly: Chios, Izmir, Istanbul, Warsaw, Rostov-on-Don, St. Petersburg. What started as a private family tree grew into a full-scale historical research.

The fate of the Avierino dynasty is a mirror of the era. We study the path of Greeks who, following Catherine II's Manifesto, left the island of Chios to start a new life in the Russian Empire. They went to build a new life in southern Russia, turning Taganrog and Odessa into prosperous ports.

If you find intersections with your family history while reading, or if you are interested in information from the documents ‚Äî please write to us. We are open to dialogue.

The family leaves their native island. First stops are the trading ports of the Ottoman Empire, where the Avierinos establish connections.

New Land

Final destinations: Mariupol and Taganrog. Here, on the shores of the Sea of Azov, the family puts down new roots.

Methodology

  • 01. Data Collection

    Searching for documents in state archives, libraries, and private collections in Greece, Turkey, and Russia.

  • 02. Validation

    Cross-checking facts: verifying birth dates with parish registers, tombstones, and indirect sources.

  • 03. Digitization

    High-resolution scanning of fragile documents and digital restoration of photographs.

  • 04. Systematization

    Building a coherent family tree and geo-referencing events to the historical map.
